I study how hormones shape within-person variation in voice, face, and other social signals across the menstrual cycle.

I am a PhD candidate in the Developmental and Evolutionary Psychology program in the Department of Psychological & Brain Sciences at UC Santa Barbara, advised by Prof. James Roney. My research focuses on hormonal correlates of within-woman shifts in attractiveness cues (voice, face, odor) and their implications for mate choice and close relationships.

I am currently completing my dissertation and will be on the academic job market / seeking postdoctoral positions.

Research

Broadly, my work sits at the intersection of evolutionary psychology and behavioral endocrinology. Ongoing projects include:

  • Voice attractiveness across the menstrual cycle – testing whether within-woman variation in vocal cues tracks ovarian hormones and fertile window timing.
  • Facial attractiveness and cycle phase – assessing subtle shifts in perceived facial attractiveness across the cycle using ranking and rating designs.
  • Daily hormonal dynamics and sociosexual psychology – modeling how day-to-day changes in estradiol and progesterone relate to desire, jealousy, and relationship-relevant emotions.

Methodological interests: mixed-effects models, ROC/AUC analyses, and meta-analytic approaches for intensive longitudinal and nested designs.
